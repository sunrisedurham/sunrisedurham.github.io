ABOUT THE DUKE ENERGY CAMPAIGN

To prevent climate disaster, we must stop using fossil fuels to power the world and build the necessary renewable energy infrastructure to transition to cleaner energy. In North Carolina, Duke Energy has a suffocating monopoly and is plowing ahead with plans to build more fossil fuel infrastructure across the state, moving us backwards instead of forward. We need to:

  1. Prevent Duke Energy from building more fossil fuel infrastructure
  2. Hold the organization to its decarbonization timeline and push it to be more ambitious
  3. Support alternative energy options in North Carolina

Our campaign consists of two mini-campaigns to achieve these goals: No Toxic Money and No New Gas.

Why do we need a No Toxic Money & No New Gas campaign? We face a two-stage problem. Fossil fuel cash corrupts the electoral process, and so lawmakers allow more natural gas usage. Our campaign will work to stop candidates from taking Duke Energy money, and thereby create a bloc of legislators free enough from fossil fuel interests to work to decarbonize our state. Join our effort to get Duke Energy cash out of North Carolina democracy, so that we can step forward together into a sustainable, green future.

NO TOXIC MONEY

Duke Energy, the company with a monopoly over North Carolina's electricity, recently released a climate plan detailing its desire to burn coal until after 2040 and natural gas indefinitely. Duke thinks that it can continue to pollute our environment with impunity because it spent its vast profits on buying-off our political representatives during the 2020 election cycle, including $1.5 million given as campaign donations and $4.3 million spent on lobbying. The GOP were 3:1 favorites for Duke's cash, but Democrats like Gov. Roy Cooper held their hats out as well.

We need a higher level of integrity from our public officials in order to face the climate emergency. We demand that North Carolina candidates and elected officials take the No Toxic Money Pledge, which is supported by the Sunrise Movement and five allied organizations.

“I will take no money or gifts from Duke Energy, Dominion Energy, or their affiliates, including from their Political Action Committees (PAC), lobbyists, and executives. In addition, I support a moratorium on all of their new fossil fuel infrastructure projects.”

Show us that you are a public servant committed to fighting the climate crisis, not a corporate sell-out who aims to entrench fossil fuels for another generation.

NO NEW GAS

In North Carolina, natural gas usage put 23 million tons of CO2 into the atmosphere. Duke Energy wants to increase gas capacity 30% by 2030 (see page 26 of the linked document). This policy courts catastrophe. Instead, we should demand No New Gas in our state: no increase in natural gas usage, and no new natural gas infrastructure.

No New Gas aims to protect our environment, which makes life possible, from further warming. Halting gas expansion also has public health, fiscal, and climate resiliency benefits as well.

In good faith, people ask, "Is this possible not to increase the amount of natural gas we use, and avoid blackouts or other side effects?"

We don't need new natural gas in buildings: Natural gas is piped into homes to power stoves, furnaces, boilers, and water heaters. All of these appliances (1, 2, 3, 4) can be electrified, or done by heat pumps (1, 2). In recognition that in-home gas use is a choice, dozens of cities have barred new construction from installing gas hook-ups.

We don't need new natural gas for electricity: 45% of Oregon's energy usage is renewable. 36% of Maine's. In North Carolina, we only get 7% of our energy from renewable sources. We don’t need more gas. We can easily meet new demand with solar farms in the Piedmont and wind farms on the Blue Ridge and off of the Outer Banks.
